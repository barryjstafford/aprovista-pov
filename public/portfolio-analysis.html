2. Commonalities Across the Portfolio

Account level
  • 74% of Apromore-touched accounts (128 of 174) have an in-flight Agentforce opp. Apromore lands in mature Salesforce accounts, not greenfield. Sales Cloud (121/174), Custom Cloud (121), Service Cloud (110), Full CRM (101), Success Cloud (114) — 6+ product families deployed.
  • MuleSoft is the most common integration wedge — 52 accounts have it installed and it's the #1 recommended add-on (12 explicit POV mentions).
  • Post-M&A harmonization is a signature pattern — 25 POVs cite it (Beretta, MPS+Carige, DNOW+MRC, CMCO+Kito, Cars.com+DealerInspire, Bendigo+RACQ, Chiesi+Amryt, HealthEquity+Inspira, Safilo+SPY+, Nine+Fairfax).
Process / use-case level
  • Customer Onboarding is the single most-mined process — 60% of POVs, spanning KYC / dealer / provider / member / broker / grower variants.
  • Regulatory & Compliance is second-largest theme — DORA (EMEA banks), CPS 230 (ANZ banks), ASIC RG 271, MiFID II, SOX, HIPAA, NDIS audit-trail evidence.
  • The top 10 themes each hit 15+ POVs — no single vertical dominates the use cases; the platform is horizontally applicable.
  • "Discovery time compression — weeks to days" is the recurring PS positioning (South East Water, British Airways references).
  • Compliance Center wedge is under-leveraged but repeatedly named — Inspira, MPS, HealthEquity, Travelers, Manulife, US Bancorp, ICE, CBA (16+ POVs).
Buyer / champion level
  • Champion tri-buyer pattern: CDAO / VP Digital Transformation (technical champion) + VP Ops / COO (process owner) + CIO / CTO (economic buyer). Shows up in nearly every T1 POV.
  • Compliance / Risk officers emerge as secondary champions in FS + Utilities specifically.
  • Salesforce AE is nearly always separate from Apromore AE — the geo split (Stuard AMER, Tapparo EMEA, van Rooij ANZ) creates a persistent joint-selling friction ("confusion over how to sell" — recurring Slack signal).
  • The "business analyst persona" is Apromore's wedge vs Celonis — KUKA's Celonis rollout failed because business users couldn't self-serve (needed SQL/PQL).
Competitor patterns
  • Celonis dominates (34 mentions) — 3 recurring anti-Celonis wedges: (1) too expensive, (2) requires SQL/developers, (3) not usable by business analysts.
  • Signavio (8) and ARIS (3) are secondary — most in EMEA + Italian banking. NAB win explicitly beat Celonis + Signavio + ARIS + Microsoft + Pega ($421K ACV / $1.58M TCV).
  • RPA / workflow platforms (UiPath, Power Automate, Pega, BluePrism) rarely compete directly — adjacent, not substitutionary.
Sales-motion patterns
  • Co-Sell with Agentforce Operations is the leading motion (55%) — Apromore-as-Discovery-Layer pattern: "mine before you automate."
  • Sell Integrated (30%) — MuleSoft (12), Service Cloud (9), Data Cloud (10), FSC (5) are the top attach products.
  • Standalone (15%) — reserved for mature process-mining buyers (Oshkosh, Leonardo, Danfoss, KUKA-style deals).
  • GA timing (Oct 2026 Hyperforce replatform) creates persistent interim-selling friction across HealthEquity, Legrand, and others in Slack.
  • 5 anchor references consistently show up: NAB (banking), KUKA (manufacturing), Oshkosh (inventory / backlog), British Airways (PS delivery), South East Water (A$5.4M business case, 64% ROI).
