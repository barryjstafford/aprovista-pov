Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. Underwriting Workbench: Submission-to-Bind Digital Twin

QBE has a $1.125M Underwriting Workbench Phase 2 opp (Stage 04) layered on FSC, Data 360, Agentforce, MuleSoft, and Marketing Cloud — the exact scaffolding a digital twin of underwriting sits on. Across 27 countries each region has its own submission triage, referral, and pricing flow bolted on Guidewire and legacy cores, so silent deviations (off-model discounts, missed referrals, out-of-appetite risks bound anyway) quietly leak margin. Apromore mines the actual submission-to-bind flow to expose referral-compliance leakage and Time-to-Quote drag — feeding the exact process steps Agentforce should automate in Phase 3. Metric moved: Time-to-Quote and referral-compliance rate.

1. APRA CPS 230 Operational Resilience Evidence Base

APRA CPS 230 came into force July 2025 and requires QBE to identify critical operations, map end-to-end dependencies, set tolerance levels, and prove operational resilience continuously — not just at annual attestation. QBE already carries a named $200k CPS 230 Operational Resilience opp. Compliance Center turns process mining into the evidence layer: continuously mined critical-operation maps, tolerance-breach detection, and third-party dependency visibility. Metric moved: audit cost per critical operation and time-to-attest.
