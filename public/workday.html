Nurture / Disqualify Analysis

1. Competitive posture. Workday competes head-to-head with Salesforce across Financial Services Cloud, HR Service Delivery (Employee Service via Slack + Data Cloud), and increasingly Sales/Service Cloud through its 2025 $1.1B Sana AI acquisition. Their agentic-AI roadmap (Workday Illuminate + Sana) is explicitly a bid to encroach on CRM turf. AFPI would be a foreign implant inside a hostile stack.

2. In-house process mining already shipping. Workday Illuminate is trained on 800B+ business transactions and mines the same Workday process substrate AFPI would target. Extending Workday with an external process-mining vendor — particularly one owned by Salesforce — is culturally and commercially implausible.

3. Data-access asymmetry. Any AFPI engagement would require Workday to hand event logs of its Procure-to-Pay, Hire-to-Retire, and Record-to-Report processes to Salesforce infrastructure. The reverse-hostage risk (competitor telemetry inside Salesforce Data Cloud) makes a lawful, mutually agreeable data-share unrealistic without an M&A-grade agreement.

4. No qualifying open pipeline. Org62 shows one open opportunity (PBO placeholder, $0) with no product attach and no AFPI-adjacent motion. There is no funded initiative, no exec sponsor, and no compliance trigger inside Salesforce’s reach.

5. What would need to change (long-term signal to monitor). A meaningful shift would require (a) Workday publicly deprecating a homegrown analytics capability, (b) a regulatory event forcing a third-party audit trail on Workday-owned processes, or (c) a partnership overture from Workday IT on shared customer telemetry — e.g. joint customers standardizing on Salesforce as system-of-engagement over Workday HCM. Route any such signal to Charlie Stuard and re-score.

Recommendation: Do not pursue. Monitor for partnership or joint-customer signals only. Any inbound must be filtered through Salesforce Competitive Intelligence before engaging.
