Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. Post-Consolidation Core Banking Harmonization

With the Adelaide Bank brand retired in December 2025 and core banking systems consolidated onto a single platform, thousands of customer accounts, product records, and transaction paths have been migrated across legacy Bendigo, legacy Adelaide, and legacy Delphi cohorts. Bendigo now needs objective evidence that the consolidated processes actually behave the same across those cohorts — variance is where migration defects hide. Apromore surfaces divergent process variants, silent reworks, and orphaned steps in weeks rather than the quarters a hand-mapped audit would take. Metric moved: 30-50% reduction in post-migration remediation tickets and a defensible operational-resilience narrative for APRA.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Core banking platform, Salesforce Service Cloud

2. RACQ Bank Acquisition Integration

Bendigo announced the RACQ Bank retail lending and deposit acquisition in December 2025, pending regulatory approval. Every merger of this size ships with two problems the executive team cannot see from dashboards: (a) which processes are truly identical and can be flipped to the acquirer’s model, and (b) where the acquired bank’s exception paths carry undocumented risk. Apromore process mining on both sides’ event logs before day-one lets the integration team target harmonization by evidence, not opinion. Metric moved: 4-8 weeks off Day-100 integration milestones and measurable cost-synergy proof for the board.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Bendigo core banking, RACQ Bank origination platform

3. Mortgage Origination Cycle-Time Reduction

Australian retail banking competes primarily on time-to-yes for mortgages, and Bendigo’s community-bank model creates handoffs between branch, credit, and central operations that dashboards routinely under-report. Apromore mines the full application-to-settlement event log, distinguishes happy-path from rework variants, and quantifies which stage — verification, valuation, or funding — actually drags the median cycle. AFPI then wraps this with prescriptive alerts and Agentforce Operations bots to nudge stuck applications back onto the fast path. Metric moved: 20-35% cycle-time compression on top-quartile mortgage variants and higher pull-through vs. major-bank benchmarks.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Mortgage origination platform, Salesforce Financial Services Cloud

Top 3 Compliance Center Use Cases

1. APRA CPS 230 Operational-Resilience Evidence

APRA CPS 230, in effect from mid-2025, requires banks to map critical operations end-to-end and evidence tolerance levels for disruption — most banks are meeting this today with static process maps that go stale within a quarter. Apromore’s Compliance Center layer turns event logs into live conformance dashboards for the critical operations Bendigo has already declared, showing regulators what actually happened rather than what was designed. That is a materially stronger control posture than a Visio artifact. Metric moved: audit-ready operational-resilience posture without adding second-line headcount, and measurable reduction in remediation-cycle drag.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Core banking, Salesforce Service Cloud

Key Contact: Ryan Brosnahan (Chief Transformation Officer)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
2. AML / KYC Onboarding Conformance Monitoring

Australian AML/CTF obligations plus AUSTRAC scrutiny make retail-bank customer onboarding one of the highest-audit-frequency processes at Bendigo. Compliance Center overlays the designed KYC control model on top of the actual onboarding event log, flags every departure from control (missing document check, out-of-order verification, unresolved risk score) in near-real-time, and produces evidence packages a regulator can walk through step-by-step. This is the same objective-evidence posture APRA expects on operational resilience, applied to financial-crime controls. Metric moved: 40-60% reduction in remediation-file preparation time and demonstrably fewer high-severity AUSTRAC findings.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: AML monitoring platform, core banking onboarding

Key Contact: Ryan Brosnahan (Chief Transformation Officer)
