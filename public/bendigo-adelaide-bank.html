Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Post-Consolidation Core Banking Harmonization

With the Adelaide Bank brand retired in December 2025 and core banking systems consolidated, thousands of customer accounts, product records, and transaction paths have been migrated onto a single platform. Bendigo now needs objective evidence that the consolidated processes actually behave the same across legacy Bendigo, legacy Adelaide, and legacy Delphi cohorts — variance is where migration defects hide. Apromore surfaces divergent process variants, silent reworks, and orphaned steps in weeks. Metric moved: 30-50% reduction in post-migration remediation tickets and a defensible operational-resilience narrative for APRA.

2. RACQ Bank Acquisition Integration

Bendigo announced the RACQ Bank retail lending/deposit acquisition in December 2025, pending regulatory approval. Every merger of this size ships with two problems the executive team cannot see from dashboards: (a) which processes are truly identical and can be flipped to the acquirer's model, and (b) where the acquired bank's exception paths carry undocumented risk. Apromore process mining on both sides' event logs before day-one lets the integration team target harmonization by evidence, not opinion. Metric moved: 4-8 weeks off Day-100 integration milestones; measurable cost synergy proof for the board.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Bendigo core banking, RACQ Bank origination platform

3. Mortgage Origination Cycle-Time Reduction

Australian retail banking competes primarily on time-to-yes for mortgages, and Bendigo's community-bank model creates handoffs between branch, credit, and central operations that dashboards routinely under-report. Apromore mines the full application-to-settlement event log, distinguishes happy-path from rework variants, and quantifies which stage — verification, valuation, funding — actually drags the median cycle. AFPI wraps this with prescriptive alerts and Agentforce Operations bots to nudge stuck applications. Metric moved: 20-35% cycle-time compression on top-quartile mortgage variants and higher pull-through vs. major-bank benchmarks.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Core banking origination, Salesforce Financial Services Cloud

Key Contact: Richard Fennell (CEO & Managing Director)

4. AML/KYC and APRA CPS 230 Operational-Resilience Evidence

APRA CPS 230 (in effect from mid-2025) requires banks to map critical operations end-to-end and evidence tolerance levels for disruption — most banks are meeting this with static process maps that go stale within a quarter. Apromore's Compliance Center layer turns event logs into live conformance dashboards for KYC onboarding, AML monitoring, and customer-remediation flows, showing regulators what actually happened rather than what was designed. Metric moved: audit-ready operational-resilience posture without adding second-line headcount; measurable reduction in remediation-cycle drag.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: AML monitoring platform, core banking onboarding

Key Contact: Ryan Brosnahan (Chief Transformation Officer)

5. Contact-Center & Complaints Handling with Agentforce Operations

Bendigo's Data Cloud footprint (prod4) and active Agentforce program with Infosys give the contact center a foundation that most peer banks lack — but the complaint-handling process still hides which cohorts of customers are stuck in the longest recovery loops.
