Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Partworks Issue Planning & Print Forecast Accuracy

De Agostini's core collectibles business ships weekly issues of long-tail series (models, cookbooks, kids' encyclopedias, etc.) into edicola and direct-subscription channels across Italy, France, UK, and Iberia. Print run over-forecasts inflate pulp write-offs on the tail; under-forecasts kill sell-through on the head. AFPI mines the plan-to-print-to-distribute event log to expose where forecasting overrides, contributor delays, and edicola allocation rules diverge from the target flow, and quantifies the actual variance-to-plan by series and market. Metric moved: pulp-write-off percentage and sell-through rate per issue.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Editorial Planning & Print Management (TBD), Salesforce Sales Cloud

Key Contact: Stefano Bordignoni (Direttore Commerciale)

2. Subscription Order-to-Fulfilment & Churn Recovery

Direct home-delivery subscriptions are the highest-margin channel but carry a heavy operational tail: missed deliveries, address changes mid-series, payment failures, and skipped-issue disputes each trigger service events that today flow through Service Cloud plus a fulfilment 3PL. AFPI reconstructs the true subscriber journey against the target renewal path, isolates the exception branches driving involuntary churn, and hands two or three variants to Agentforce for automated recovery. Metric moved: subscription completion rate and involuntary churn on active series.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Service Cloud, Fulfilment / 3PL WMS (TBD)

Key Contact: Tiziana Scafetta (IT CRM, Sale & Distribution Manager)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
3. Edicola Returns & Reverse-Logistics Reconciliation

Unsold newsstand copies flow back through a distributor-managed reverse-logistics chain and must be counted, credited, and destroyed under Italian consumer law. Reconciliation lags and credit disputes with distributors are a chronic drag on cash and on the finance close. AFPI mines the returns event log (dispatch, receipt, count, credit note, destruction) to expose distributor-specific bottlenecks and multi-hand-off loops, and gives finance and commercial a shared conformance view. Metric moved: returns-cycle days and credit-note dispute rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Distributor Returns Portal (TBD), Finance / Credit-Note ledger (TBD)

Key Contact: Rudi Ghirelli (IT Demand Manager / IT Supply Chain Manager)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
4. Procure-to-Pay for Print, Paper & Collectible Componentry

Every partworks series carries a bill of materials — paper, ink, plastic collectible parts (models, figurines, minerals), and print capacity — sourced against tight editorial calendars. Late supplier confirmations and expedited spot buys are a recurring margin leak that shows up in the Direttore Acquisti's books but rarely in the CFO's dashboards. AFPI mines P2P from requisition to invoice-clear, exposes the maverick-buy variants, and quantifies avoidable rush costs by supplier and series. Metric moved: expedited-freight spend and on-time supplier confirmation rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: ERP P2P (TBD), Supplier Portal (TBD)

Key Contact: Silvia Riccardi (Direttore Acquisti)

5. Digital & DeA Scuola Content Publishing Pipeline

The digital-learning arm (DeA Scuola, DeA app catalogue) runs a parallel content pipeline from author brief through editorial QA, digital packaging, and platform release — a very different tempo from partworks but the same conformance question: where does the target release calendar diverge from reality? AFPI mines editorial-workflow event data to expose stalls at rights clearance, QA, and localisation hand-offs, and quantifies the drag each variant puts on release-date slippage. Metric moved: content release on-time rate and rework loops per title.
