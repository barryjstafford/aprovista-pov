Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Core Banking to Financial Services Cloud migration readiness. The $15M SAP Core Banking → FSC program (Phases 1 & 3) is exactly the kind of multi-year re-platforming where AFPI de-risks the transition by mining the current SAP order-to-cash and loan-origination flows before cutover. Pain: every core-banking migration overruns on unknown process variants (undocumented branch workarounds, dual-entry rework). AFPI plays the "as-is" discovery layer that feeds the FSC to-be design, and it moves the metric that matters — cutover slippage weeks and post-go-live rework hours. Engage Julie Levesque (EVP Technology & Operations) as champion.

2. Digital lending origination cycle time. Phase 3 of the SAP→FSC build is specifically Digital Lending — a domain where funnel drop-off, credit-decision rework, and manual document chase are the top three time sinks. AFPI mines the loan-app event log to expose where applications stall (income verification, KYC re-work, adjudicator queue) and quantifies the automation ROI before Agentforce or FSC Lending workflows are configured. Moves origination cycle time from days to hours and lifts pull-through rate. Champion: the Commercial & Personal Banking CDO — engage Lucie Blanchet (EVP Personal & Commercial Banking).

3. Wealth Advisor onboarding & servicing automation. The open Wealth Advisor Portal Automation opp ($120K, Stage 3) is a natural AFPI wedge: mine the advisor task-completion event log (client onboarding, KYC refresh, portfolio rebalancing hand-offs) to isolate the 5-8 repetitive multi-system steps that account for 60%+ of advisor admin time. AFPI + Agentforce turns that into an agent-executed workflow with human-in-the-loop review. Metric: advisor productive hours per week, and time-to-first-trade for new HNW clients. Engage Nancy Paquet (EVP Wealth Management).

4. Contact center / Agentic Omni Gateway grounding. The Agentic Omni Gateway opp ($150K) points to an omni-channel service redesign. AFPI provides the process-mined baseline (call reasons, hand-off patterns, first-contact-resolution gaps) that Agentforce agents need as grounding — without it, agents automate the wrong thing. Pain: today's IVR + agent hand-offs create 20-30% repeat contacts; regulator-driven complaint SLAs (FCAC in Canada) make this a compliance issue, not just a cost one. Moves FCR, repeat-contact rate, and complaint aging. Champion: Marie Chantal Gingras (SVP Customer Experience) — validate title.

5. Regulatory reporting & AML process compliance (Compliance Center wedge). As a Canadian D-SIB regulated by OSFI plus FINTRAC AML obligations, NBC runs heavy periodic-review, transaction-monitoring, and adverse-media investigation processes across SAP, FSC (future), and third-party AML tools. AFPI Compliance Center mines these flows to prove control effectiveness, isolate SLA-breach root causes, and produce audit-ready process evidence for OSFI E-13 and FINTRAC reviews. Moves case-closure SLA, false-positive rework, and audit prep hours. Engage Bill Bonney (Chief Compliance Officer) — validate title.
