Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Order-to-Delivery for Custom Trucks. Daimler Truck's build-to-order model spans thousands of chassis, powertrain, and body-builder configurations across Mercedes-Benz Trucks, Freightliner, Western Star, FUSO, and BharatBenz. Long lead times, missed customer promise dates, and finished-goods write-downs are chronic pain — and CFO scrutiny is intense post-IPO. AFPI mines the SAP order and MES event logs to expose where variants stall, which options trigger rework, and which plants consistently beat vs. miss cycle time; the wedge is a 3-4 week diagnostic that pairs with the Customer 360 Data Cloud opp to unify order status across SAP, dealer DMS, and CRM. Metric moved: on-time delivery to promise date +8-12pp, working-capital tied up in WIP -5%. Engage Karin Rådström (CEO) and the head of Mercedes-Benz Trucks Operations.

2. Aftersales & Warranty Claims Handling. Warranty is the single largest controllable cost lever in commercial vehicles — every day a claim sits in dealer queues drags NPS and cash. Today the process spans dealer portals, ServiceNow tickets, and SAP warranty modules with heavy manual triage. AFPI process-mines the claim event log to find rework loops, dealer outliers, and codes that consistently escalate to engineering, then feeds an Agentforce Service Agent (see the Agentic Pilot and ServiceNow Integration opps) to auto-triage and route. Metric moved: warranty cycle time -30-40%, warranty leakage -3-5%. Engage Achim Puchert (CEO, Mercedes-Benz Trucks) and the aftersales director.

3. Dealer & Own-Retail Service Operations. The Daimler Truck Own Retail Service opp is a direct AFPI wedge — as Daimler Truck expands company-owned retail (RoadStar in Europe, Rush/Daimler Truck North America JVs), it needs a single view of service-bay throughput, technician utilization, and parts-availability across hundreds of locations. AFPI mines the service-order event log, exposes idle-bay time, parts-wait bottlenecks, and appointment-to-close cycle time, and hands the fix to Agentforce for Service. Metric moved: service-bay utilization +10pp, parts-fill-rate on first visit +15pp. Engage Karl Deppen (CEO Trucks Asia) and the Own Retail program lead in EMEA.

4. Procure-to-Pay & Supplier Onboarding. With 20,000+ direct suppliers across four brands and a full BEV/H2 supplier stand-up underway, PO exception rates, invoice mismatches, and supplier-onboarding lead time bleed working capital and delay new programs. AFPI mines the SAP P2P event log to pinpoint the 15-20% of vendors driving 80% of exceptions and the maverick-buying paths bypassing framework agreements — a classic Regrello-adjacent wedge that expands the Regrello (Upside) Shadow Oppty. Metric moved: touchless invoice rate 60% → 80%+, supplier onboarding cycle -40%. Engage Eva Scherer (CFO) and the head of Global Procurement.

5. B2B Fleet Sales & Contact-Center Deflection. The B2B CRM and Contact Center opps line up perfectly: fleet buyers move slowly through configure-price-quote across dozens of hand-offs (sales, financing via Daimler Truck Financial Services, upfit body-builders, regulatory). AFPI mines the CPQ + case event log to expose stalled quotes, escalation paths, and repeat-caller drivers, then feeds an Agentforce Service Agent to deflect Tier-1 fleet inquiries. Metric moved: quote-to-order cycle -25%, contact-center deflection to agent 40%+. Engage John O'Leary (CEO, Daimler Truck North America) and the head of Fleet Sales EMEA.
