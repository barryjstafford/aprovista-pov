Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. Post-Humm-Acquisition Collections Harmonization

Credit Corp's takeover of Humm Group folds a BNPL and commercial-lending book onto its debt-purchase engine — leaving two collections stacks and two contact strategies to converge. This is the classic post-M&A trap where cycle time and cost-to-collect drift for 12–18 months while ops teams pick a target-state variant by committee. AFPI mines the as-is collections flow from both entities side-by-side and surfaces the shortest-path variant to standardize on, with rework hotspots called out. Metric: cost per dollar collected, target 6–10% reduction inside two quarters.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Service Cloud, Tableau

Key Contact: Matthew Angell (Group COO — Industry Leadership & Acquisitions)

2. Purchased Debt Ledger (PDL) Onboarding to First-Payment

Every new PDL tranche — the core of Credit Corp's model — must be ingested, segmented, and first-contacted quickly to hit the collection curve. Delays between ledger settlement and first productive contact are the single biggest driver of miss-rate. AFPI mines the ledger-load-to-first-payment sequence across Service Cloud cases and Tableau segments, exposing where segmentation, channel choice, or dialer routing lose days. Metric: days-to-first-payment on new ledgers, target −25%.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Service Cloud, Tableau

3. Consumer Lending Origination-to-Fund (Wallet Wizard / ClearCash)

Wallet Wizard and ClearCash push small-amount credit through a heavily automated origination flow, but rework loops in ID verification and responsible-lending checks quietly tax the funnel. Mining the origination event log exposes where applications stall, drop, or loop — and how those variants correlate with default rates downstream. AFPI plus the open $130k Document AI opp closes the loop between doc extraction and process. Metric: time-to-fund, target 20–30% reduction on straight-through cases.
