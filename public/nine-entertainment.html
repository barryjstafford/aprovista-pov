Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. Post-QMS Merger Order-to-Cash Harmonization (Broadcast + OOH)

Nine acquired QMS (outdoor advertising) for A$850M in early 2026, adding a physical OOH inventory book alongside broadcast, publishing, and digital. Order-to-cash today runs across Nine's ad-ops stack (Operative/Freewheel) plus QMS's own OOH scheduling and billing — a classic post-M&A control gap. Mining the combined O2C flow surfaces revenue leakage, duplicate holds, and days-to-invoice drift that Finance cannot see from either standalone system. Metrics moved: DSO, invoice-accuracy rate, revenue-leakage %.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Operative, Salesforce Media Cloud

Key Contact: Matt Stanton (CEO, ex-CFO — owns integration synergy)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
2. Cross-Platform Ad Inventory-to-Delivery Reconciliation

A single advertiser campaign at Nine spans linear TV, 9Now streaming, publishing (SMH/Age/AFR), and now QMS OOH — each with its own sell-side stack and delivery logs. Ad-ops teams manually reconcile sold vs. delivered vs. billed impressions, and make-goods are stitched together in spreadsheets. Mining the delivery flow across ad servers, CDN logs, and billing exposes where impressions are lost or under-delivered before the client escalates. Metrics moved: make-good %, revenue-recognition speed, under-delivery rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Google Ad Manager, Freewheel

Key Contact: Ben Campbell (Chief Digital Officer)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
3. Divestiture Carve-Out & TSA Exit (Radio, NBN TV, Domain)

In 2026 Nine is exiting Radio (to the Laundy family), NBN Television (to WIN), and its 60% Domain stake (to CoStar) — three simultaneous carve-outs, each with Transition Services Agreements covering finance, HR, and IT. Every day a TSA runs longer than plan is a direct P&L hit. Mining shared-services flows (payroll, AP, IT ticketing, license entitlements) tells the CFO exactly which entity is still consuming shared capacity and where the cutovers can safely land. Metrics moved: TSA exit-days, shared-service unit cost, stranded-cost %.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: ServiceNow, Workday

Key Contact: Matt Stanton (CEO)

Top 3 Task Mining Use Cases

1. Ad-Ops Traffic Coordinator Desktop Swivel-Chair

Nine's traffic coordinators flip between Operative, Freewheel, Google Ad Manager, Media Cloud, and email/attachments to book, traffic, and reconcile a single advertiser campaign. Task mining captures each coordinator's on-screen actions to isolate copy/paste patterns and repeated lookups across ad-ops apps that Agentforce Operations could automate. Feeds a direct co-sell business case with the in-flight Agentforce opp. Metrics moved: touch-time per booking, campaign-book cycle time, coordinator FTE per revenue $.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Operative, Google Ad Manager

2. Agentforce-for-Adsales Agent Handoff & Seller Wrap

The $1.13M Agentforce for Adsales opp will hand triage, media-plan generation, and quote turnaround to an AI agent — but sellers still need to close, and their post-agent wrap on the Sales Cloud console is where cycle time hides. Top 3 Compliance Center Use Cases

1. ACMA Broadcast & Editorial Standards Controls

Nine's TV and radio operations are governed by ACMA codes covering advertising standards, classification, news accuracy, and complaints handling — each with defined response SLAs and evidence obligations. Compliance Center codifies complaint-intake through response as a controlled workflow with segregation of duties, sign-offs, and continuous evidence for the regulator. Especially valuable as generative-AI tools enter newsroom and adsales workflows. Metrics moved: complaint-response SLA hit-rate, code-breach incidents, audit-evidence prep time.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: ServiceNow, editorial CMS

Key Contact: General Counsel & Company Secretary (Nine Entertainment)

2. Privacy Act / Consent & Data-Handling Evidence (Data Cloud)

Nine consolidates subscriber and audience data from Stan, 9Now, and publishing paywalls into Data Cloud — and Australia's Privacy Act reforms plus mandatory data-breach reporting raise the evidence bar sharply.
