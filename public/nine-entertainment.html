Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Post-QMS Merger Order-to-Cash Harmonization (Broadcast + OOH)

Nine acquired QMS (outdoor advertising) for A$850M in early 2026, bringing a physical OOH inventory book alongside broadcast, publishing, and digital. Order-to-cash today runs across Nine's ad-ops stack (Operative/Freewheel) plus QMS's own OOH scheduling and billing systems — a classic post-M&A control gap. Mining the combined O2C flow surfaces revenue leakage, duplicate holds, and days-to-invoice drift that Finance cannot see from either standalone system. Metrics moved: DSO, invoice-accuracy rate, revenue-leakage %.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Operative, Salesforce Media Cloud

2. Agentforce-for-Adsales Agent Handoff Optimization

Nine has a $1.13M Agentforce for Adsales opportunity in Stage 03 (Validating Benefits & Value) — an obvious co-sell wedge for AFPI. Once agents are handling triage, media-plan generation, and quote turnaround, the business needs conformance checks: which conversations hand off to human sellers, where deals stall, and whether the agent shortens the RFP-to-order cycle vs. control. Mining the Agentforce event log against CRM stage transitions gives Nine (and the Salesforce account team) hard proof of agent ROI. Metrics moved: agent deflection rate, RFP-to-order cycle time, seller productivity.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Agentforce, Salesforce Sales Cloud

3. Cross-Platform Ad Inventory-to-Delivery Reconciliation

A single advertiser campaign at Nine spans linear TV, 9Now streaming, publishing (SMH/Age/AFR), and now QMS OOH — each with its own sell-side stack and delivery logs. Ad-ops teams manually reconcile "sold vs. delivered vs. billed" impressions and spots, and make-goods are stitched together in spreadsheets. Mining the delivery flow across ad servers, CDN logs, and billing surfaces the exact points where impressions are lost or under-delivered before the client escalates. Metrics moved: make-good %, revenue recognition speed, campaign under-delivery rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Google Ad Manager, Freewheel

Key Contact: Ben Campbell (Chief Digital Officer)

4. Divestiture Carve-Out & TSA Exit (Radio, NBN TV, Domain)

In 2026 Nine is exiting Radio (to the Laundy family), NBN Television (to WIN), and its 60% Domain stake (to CoStar) — three simultaneous carve-outs, each with Transition Services Agreements (TSAs) covering finance, HR, and IT processes. Every day a TSA runs longer than plan is a direct P&L hit. Process mining the shared services flows (payroll, AP, IT ticketing, license entitlements) tells the CFO exactly which entity is still consuming shared capacity and where the carve-out cutovers can safely land. Metrics moved: TSA exit-days, shared-service unit cost, stranded-cost %.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: ServiceNow, Workday (finance/HR)

Key Contact: Matt Stanton (CEO)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
5. Subscriber Lifecycle Mining Across Stan, 9Now & Publishing Paywalls

Nine runs three distinct subscription/registration flows — Stan (SVOD), 9Now (AVOD with registration), and publishing paywalls (SMH/Age/AFR) — all landing consented data into Data Cloud. Business questions like "where do churn-risk subscribers actually drop off?" and "which retention offer path shortens win-back?" require mining the real customer-journey event log, not just aggregate funnels. Apromore on top of Data Cloud gives the growth team variant analysis and conformance in one no-code view. Metrics moved: churn rate, LTV, win-back conversion.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Data Cloud, Salesforce Media Cloud
