Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. NeoGames / Interactive Post-Acquisition Process Harmonization

Aristocrat closed the ~US$1.2B NeoGames acquisition in April 2024 to stand up Aristocrat Interactive as its online real-money gaming arm. The integration is still merging billing, operator onboarding, KYC, and content-release processes across two very different tech stacks. Mining the actual flows in Interactive against the legacy Aristocrat Gaming baseline exposes duplicate approval loops, variant explosion in KYC, and where operator-facing SLAs are slipping — the classic PMI value moment where a 2–4 week Apromore analyst-led discovery beats a 12-week Celonis SI engagement. Metric moved: cycle time from operator signature to live content, and % of KYC cases resolved on the first pass.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: NeoGames back-office, Salesforce (Interactive operator accounts)

2. EGM Order-to-Cash: Sydney & Las Vegas Manufacturing to Casino Floor

Aristocrat runs a dual-hub electronic gaming machine (EGM) manufacturing footprint — Sydney design/build for ANZ/EMEA, Las Vegas assembly and distribution for the Americas — delivering into a heavily regulated casino-operator customer base. The O2C process (quote, jurisdictional certification, build, ship, install, revenue recognition) has notorious variance because every state and tribal jurisdiction adds its own certification wait state. Apromore quantifies where the certification bottleneck actually lives and lets ops simulate parallel-track approvals; the metric that moves is days from order to installed-and-revenue-recognized. This dovetails directly with the $254k Agentforce-for-Manufacturing opp already in flight.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: ERP/manufacturing system, Salesforce (opportunity/install)

Key Contact: Claire Mileto (Director of Operations, Oz Group of Studios)

The $670k “Aristocrat Australia ServiceNow Replacement - 501 (ITSM, PnC, Finance)” opp is a live re-platforming across three service domains at once.
