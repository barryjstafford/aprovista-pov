MetLife's Group Benefits segment (dental, disability, absence management, voluntary) has publicly declared claims automation and adjudication speed a "New Frontier" strategic priority. AFPI mines the claims event log — intake → triage → medical review → adjudication → payment — to expose where cases stall (missing docs, provider callbacks, adjuster rework) versus where they can be routed straight to an Agentforce claims agent. This is the classic two-week pilot: no PQL specialist, one Ops analyst finds the top three broken variants and hands them to Agentforce Operations. 2. Underwriting Cycle Time — Life & Annuities

Individual life and annuity underwriting is a multi-desk process (application → medical evidence → APS retrieval → risk classification → policy issuance) where every day of delay puts placement at risk to a competitor. AFPI overlays the actual underwriting event log to distinguish "waiting on the APS" from "waiting on internal rework" — a distinction MetLife's own underwriters can rarely quantify today. The wedge lands directly against the active $565K MS-MetLife CTO opportunity as a Discovery-phase Signature Success deliverable. Metric moved: app-to-issue cycle time and NIGO (not-in-good-order) rate.
