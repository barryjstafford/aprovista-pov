Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Quote-to-Cash Harmonization Across Sales Cloud, CPQ, and Revenue Cloud

Workiva is mid-transition from SteelBrick CPQ (807 seats) onto Revenue Cloud (open $700k opp). That migration is the highest-risk process in the account — approval loops, discount overrides, and manual re-quotes tend to explode during CPQ platform swaps. Mining the current quote-to-order-to-cash event log surfaces every rework loop and non-standard path before cutover, so the Revenue Cloud rollout starts from a cleansed process baseline. Metric moved: quote cycle time and % first-pass-clean orders.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce CPQ, Sales Cloud

Key Contact: Jill Klindt (CFO)

2. Subscription Renewal & Retention Lifecycle

A $3.35M Full-CRM/CPQ/Org-Permission renewal is already staged for FY28, and a $674k Slack renewal sits alongside it. For a SaaS whose entire P&L rides on renewal rates, the renewal-motion process — health-score triggers, CSM outreach, upsell timing, executive escalation — is the single biggest revenue-protecting flow to mine. AFPI reveals where renewals silently slip into "at risk" and which CSM plays actually correlate with saves. Metric moved: gross retention and % renewals closed >60 days early.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Sales Cloud, Slack

3. Customer Onboarding & Time-to-First-Filing

Workiva's value only lands when a customer files their first SEC report, ESG disclosure, or SOX narrative on the platform — everything before that is at-risk revenue. The onboarding process today spans Sales handoff, Signature Success ($300k opp), implementation, and enablement over Slack channels. Mining the end-to-end handoff exposes stalled implementations, ghost tasks, and where enablement content doesn't translate into product adoption. Metric moved: time-to-first-filing and 90-day product-usage rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Sales Cloud, Slack

Key Contact: Chief Customer Officer (Customer Success & Services)

4. Internal Financial Close & SOX ICFR Execution

Workiva itself is a public NYSE:WK filer whose own finance team lives the exact pain their product solves. The internal financial close, SOX narrative sign-off, and ICFR control-testing workflow is a high-signal, high-executive-visibility process — and the CFO gets to prove "we run our own house on process intelligence." AFPI shows late-cycle bottlenecks in accrual reviews, control evidence collection, and management review sign-offs, and where automation could take work off the shoulders of the close team.
