Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Lead-to-Install Attribution (Web → CEC → TrexPro → Dealer)

Trex's 4.6M-record lead database moves through the website, CEC, TrexPro referral network, and Lowe's/Home Depot POS before an install closes the loop. AFPI mines the actual event log — form submission, CEC touch, TrexPro accept/decline, dealer purchase — to prove which paths convert and where high-intent consumers stall. That gives Agentforce a documented as-is process to route against instead of guessing, and lets Trex quote a defensible conversion rate to its dealer partners for the first time. Metric moved: verified conversion rate per lead source, cost per confirmed install.

2. CEC Case Triage & Agentforce Deflection

The Consumer Experience Center is overwhelmed seasonally and handles a heavy tail of "where is my dealer / warranty / cleaning" repeat inquiries that the Agentforce north-star targets for auto-response. AFPI mines the Service Cloud case log to expose which inquiry types actually dominate volume, which escalate to human agents, and where installation-defect cases loop back — sizing the deflection prize before the agent goes live. This is the Apromore edge: a single no-code analyst view of every case variant, replacing a two-specialist SQL+PQL model. Metric moved: CEC case volume deflected, average handle time, repeat-contact rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Service Cloud, Agentforce

3. TrexPro Contractor Onboarding & Lead-Acceptance Flow

The TrexPro program is the physical delivery layer of the digital funnel — contractors receive real-time high-intent lead notifications and must accept quickly for the model to work. AFPI mines the PRM/Partner Community event log to show where contractors stall in onboarding, which regions have low accept-rates, and how quickly accepted leads convert to booked installs. That directly feeds the Manufacturing Cloud UE + Channel Rev Management opp already in flight. Metric moved: TrexPro accept-rate SLA, lead-to-install cycle time.

4. Dealer & Distribution Order-to-Cash

Trex ships composite decking through Lowe's, Home Depot, and regional lumber yards on a seasonal demand curve where stockout at the shelf directly hits consumer NPS. AFPI mines the OTC event log — dealer order → allocation → ship-from-plant (Winchester VA, Fernley NV, Little Rock AR) → invoice — to reveal fill-rate gaps, allocation overrides, and margin leakage on rush orders. This anchors the Red Carpet MFG Cloud + Channel Rev Management build in a measured baseline. Metric moved: dealer fill rate, perfect-order %, DSO.

5. Procure-to-Pay & Recycled-Material Sourcing

Trex's core differentiation is a manufacturing process built on reclaimed polyethylene and waste wood fibers, giving strategic sourcing an unusually complex supplier landscape versus a typical PVF distributor. AFPI mines the P2P event log — PR → PO → GR → invoice — to expose duplicate suppliers, off-catalog spend on virgin resin, and manual approval loops that quietly erode input-cost advantage. Feeding a data-cleanup adjacency into any future MDM conversation without competing with it. Metric moved: PO cycle time, off-contract spend %, recycled-content sourcing throughput.
