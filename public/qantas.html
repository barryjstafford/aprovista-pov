Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Safety & Risk Case-Management Cycle Time

Every safety report, hazard, and incident at Qantas Group must be triaged, investigated, and closed under CASA and IOSA audit windows. Today those cases flow through multiple hand-offs across flight ops, engineering, and cabin safety with limited end-to-end visibility. AFPI can mine the full case lifecycle to expose reopens, aged queues, and investigator bottlenecks, then trigger Agentforce actions to route, escalate, and close cases faster. Target metric: median case cycle time and % reopened after closure. This use case is already funded via the live Apromore Safety & Risk opp.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Service Cloud, MuleSoft

2. On-Time Departure & Turnaround Sequence

Turnaround is a choreography of fuelling, catering, cleaning, boarding, and dispatch — dozens of dependent steps that ripple into delay codes when any actor slips. Mining the actual event sequence per aircraft rotation exposes where the median turnaround hides its longest tail, and which airport or ground-handler is the systemic offender. AFPI can then feed Agentforce Operations with realtime nudges to gate agents and crew. Metric: on-time-departure % (D0/D15) and average turnaround minutes by port.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Sabre/Departure Control, ServiceNow

Key Contact: VP Airline Operations (VP Airline Operations)

3. Irregular Operations (IROps) & Disruption Recovery

Cancellations, diversions, and weather events force a scramble across crew rescheduling, aircraft swap, hotel & ground transport, and passenger re-accommodation. Slack signal shows Qantas is already tackling "Disruption SMS Consolidation" ($370k opp). Apromore can mine the disruption playbook end-to-end to prove where recovery time is lost and which cohorts of passengers are repeatedly under-served. Metric: hours from event to full recovery, and Net Promoter recovery after a disruption.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud

4. Fuel-Conservation Behavioural Compliance Loop

Jodi Smith directly named this as a Qantas strategy-lead priority in June 2026: getting pilots, cabin crew, and ground staff to consistently execute fuel-saving procedures during taxi, climb, cruise, and descent. Much of the work is behavioural and offline, but each behaviour leaves a digital trace in flight-data, dispatch, and crew-briefing systems. AFPI can mine the compliance loop — briefed → executed → measured → coached — and pair it with Agentforce nudges. Metric: kg of fuel saved per sector vs plan.
