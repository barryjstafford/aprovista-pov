Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Work-Order Turn & Repair Cycle Time (Field Service)

Hunt runs 876+ Field Service Plus techs plus a large contractor pool across ~50 military installations, and turn-time on unit make-ready and resident repair is the single loudest MHPI KPI. Mining the Work Order → Service Appointment → Technician Dispatch → Complete cycle exposes rework, cross-base dispatch waste, and contractor SLA breach patterns. AFPI can quantify the hours lost per rework loop and simulate scheduling policy changes before they hit the field. Metric moved: median unit-turn days and first-time-fix rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Field Service, Service Cloud

2. Move-In / Move-Out & Lease Turnover

PCS-driven turnover creates spiky move-in / move-out volume that has to hand off cleanly between community managers, maintenance, contractors, and billing. Mining the lease turnover process end-to-end surfaces bottlenecks between inspection, make-ready, and re-occupancy, and shows where variance across bases is driving vacancy loss. AFPI's variant analysis lets Hunt see which portfolio operations directors are running the process cleanly and which aren't. Metric moved: days-vacant per unit and turn cost variance.

3. Resident Service Case & Call-Center Deflection

Hunt has 1,585 Call Center seats and 15k+ Customer Community logins fielding resident complaints — a documented reputational pain point for privatized military housing. Mining the case-to-resolution flow shows which reason codes drive repeat contacts, where the community-manager escalation path adds days, and where an Agentforce agent could deflect routine tickets. AFPI ties the ROI of an Agentforce deployment to concrete conformance gaps rather than a demo. Metric moved: repeat-contact rate and mean-time-to-resolve.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Service Cloud, Customer Communities

4. Preventive Maintenance & Compliance Inspections

MHPI compliance obligations (HUD, DoD, environmental, lead/asbestos, life-safety) require documented preventive-maintenance and inspection cadences per unit and per base. Mining the scheduled-inspection → completion → remediation flow reveals bases running behind cadence and remediation loops that stall. This becomes the Compliance-Center wedge — a defensible audit trail plus early-warning conformance dashboards for the Congressional-scrutiny reality Hunt lives with. Metric moved: on-time inspection compliance and open-remediation age.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Field Service, MuleSoft (integration to source systems)

Hunt operates a large third-party contractor network layered on top of its in-house technicians, meaning the same work order can traverse multiple ownership and payment paths. Mining the contractor lifecycle — onboarding → dispatch → completion → invoice → pay — highlights slow onboarding, dispatch-to-wrong-tier waste, and invoice cycle-time.
