Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. Consumer Onboarding & KYC/AML Cycle Time

Chase Consumer & Community Banking onboards millions of retail and small-business customers per year through a fragmented KYC/AML workflow that spans branch, digital, and back-office review queues. Apromore mines the actual account-opening event log to expose rework loops, manual escalations, and stalled cases that quietly push regulatory SLAs. The play plugs directly into the existing Agentforce Flex Credit footprint so Chase pays for outcomes, not implementation. Metric: median onboarding cycle time and first-touch-resolution rate.

2. Wholesale Payments & Wire Exception Handling

J.P. Morgan Payments processes roughly $10T per day; every wire that lands in exception review is a manual cost and a customer-experience risk. Apromore mines the exception queue to distinguish repeatable failure patterns (data quality, sanctions hit, cutoff timing) from true one-offs, then routes the repeatable ones to Agentforce Operations. This is the clearest 2-4 week time-to-value story vs. a specialist SQL/PQL platform — one no-code analyst inside Payments Ops can pilot. Metric: exception-clear rate per FTE and STP percentage.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Payments platform (TBD), Agentforce

3. Commercial & Investment Bank Deal Lifecycle

The end-to-end path from pitch → mandate → syndication → booking spans dozens of internal systems and every desk runs its own variant. Apromore reconstructs the real process from CRM, deal-management, and settlement events to show where mandates leak, where legal review re-queues, and where booking breaks. Value framing against TPL: the process-mining tier reveals the top three revenue-leak paths inside a quarter, well under a single lost mandate. Metric: mandate-to-book cycle time and revenue leakage per stage.
