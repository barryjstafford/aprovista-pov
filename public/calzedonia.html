Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. Omnichannel Order-to-Cash Across 15+ Brands

Oniverse runs 15+ brands (Calzedonia, Intimissimi, Tezenis, Falconeri, Signorvino, Cantiere del Pardo, and more) across e-commerce and 5,700+ stores in 59 countries on a shared Commerce Cloud + Order Management backbone. Each brand runs its own O2C variant, creating hidden rework loops and inconsistent SLAs at scale. Apromore mines every order event across brands and countries, quantifies variance versus the "one Oniverse" target flow, and hands exception routing to Agentforce Operations. Metric moved: O2C cycle time, first-pass yield, and manual-touch rate per order.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Commerce Cloud, Salesforce Order Management

Key Contact: Frederico Succi (Head of IT Omnichannel, Oniverse)

2. Returns & Reverse-Logistics Optimization

Specialty apparel — hosiery, lingerie, cashmere — carries the highest return rates in retail, and Oniverse absorbs the cost across 59 countries with mixed carrier and store-return paths. Today exception handling is a black box: refund-delay complaints hit Service Cloud with no upstream root-cause visibility. Apromore mines the return event stream from OMS, WMS, and carrier events, isolates the top drivers of refund delay, and gives Ops a variant-level view before Agentforce Return Management scales further. Metric moved: refund cycle time, return-contact rate, and process-leakage shrink.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Order Management, Salesforce Service Cloud

Key Contact: Head of Customer Operations, Oniverse (TBD — field verify)

3. Store Replenishment & Allocation

With 5,700+ stores across 15+ brands and highly seasonal SKUs (swimwear, hosiery, cashmere), replenishment cycle time directly drives full-price sell-through. Today the process spans ERP, OMS, and store systems — allocation decisions are made outside any mined baseline. Apromore mines the replenishment event log, exposes stockout-to-store-request loops, and quantifies the working-capital cost of slow allocations across brand hierarchies. Metric moved: stockout rate, full-price sell-through, and days-of-cover variance per brand.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Order Management, ERP (SAP/Oracle)
