Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Cancellation & Refund Cycle Time. Refund and cancellation flows span the traveler, Expedia, and 100+ supplier partners (airlines, hotels, car rentals), with SLA-driven escalations that spike CSAT complaints when partner response drifts. Mining the event log across Salesforce Service Cloud, the supplier API layer, and payments will surface the true rework loops, partner-side bottlenecks, and refund-aging buckets that consume agent time. AFPI ties directly to the active MCA Phase 1/2 spend by giving Agentforce the ground-truth process map to route and act on. Metric moved: refund cycle time and Tier-2 escalation rate. Champion: Jochen Koedijk (Chief Customer Officer).

2. Partner & Supply Onboarding (Property/Air). Onboarding a new hotel, alternative-accommodation host, or air partner runs through content ingestion, rate/inventory mapping, contracting, and compliance checks — typically weeks of back-and-forth email plus custom internal tooling. Process mining exposes where properties stall in the funnel (missing content, tax setup, geo tagging) and how long time-to-first-booking really takes vs. the internal SLA. AFPI plus Agentforce can auto-nudge partners at the exact stall points and auto-approve low-risk paths. Metric moved: partner time-to-live and inventory ramp velocity. Champion: Ariane Gorin (CEO, ex-Expedia for Business).

3. Traveler Support Contact-Deflection Loop. Millions of calls and chats hit Service Cloud each year with predictable patterns — itinerary changes, refund status, price-drop disputes, loyalty issues — but true root causes hide inside the process, not the transcript. Mining the case journey (self-service → virtual agent → human) shows which policies and system handoffs actually cause repeat contacts. AFPI feeds Agentforce Service Agents the deflection blueprint per case type; wedge is Agentforce Ops co-sell attached to the two live MCA opps. Metric moved: contained rate and cost-to-serve per booking. Champion: Jochen Koedijk (Chief Customer Officer).

4. Marketing Cloud Journey & Personalization Compliance. The MCA PLS engagements are already building personalized landing solutions across Expedia, Hotels.com, and Vrbo brands — but journey execution across email, push, and paid inevitably drifts from consent/segmentation rules, especially post-GDPR/CCPA. AFPI mines the actual send-to-conversion journey to catch delivery leaks, consent-block silent failures, and slow segment build times. This is the cleanest “Compliance Center wedge” and lands inside the current MCA project team. Metric moved: campaign lead time and consent-audit exceptions. Champion: Jennifer Andre (SVP, Global Business Services / advertising & media).

5. Order-to-Cash on the B2B (Expedia TAAP / Expedia for Business) Book. The B2B Commerce footprint powers agent-partner and corporate-travel bookings where invoicing, chargebacks, and settlement disputes create hidden aging. Mining the order-to-cash process across Salesforce and the finance stack surfaces where disputes originate, which partner segments carry the aging, and where auto-write-off thresholds should sit. AFPI gives finance ops the audit-grade process view that supports Agentforce automation in dispute triage. Metric moved: DSO on partner book and dispute cycle time. Champion: Scott Schenkel (CFO).
