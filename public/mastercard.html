Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Ethoca Chargeback & Dispute Resolution — Prove the ISV OEM Value Before the OEM Contract Closes

The Ethoca OEM Native ISV opp is in Assess, which means the SF/Mastercard team is still building the "why now" narrative for embedding Ethoca into Financial Services Cloud. AFPI mines the actual dispute lifecycle event log — issuer intake, Ethoca alert, merchant refund path, network representment — to expose which variants leak days and dollars, then hands the top three broken paths to Agentforce Operations to automate. This turns the OEM story from "another data feed" into "measurable cycle-time and write-off reduction," and does it in the two-week timeframe an ISV team can absorb. Metric: dispute-to-resolution days, first-cycle refund rate, chargeback write-off $.

2. FSC Data Cloud Pilot — Add a Process-Lineage Layer for Issuer Analytics

The Mastercard FSC Data Cloud ISV opp is at the earliest stage; the fastest way to accelerate it is to give the pilot a use case that only Data Cloud + AFPI can solve. AFPI overlays process lineage on the issuer-servicing event stream (auth → decline → chargeback → retention save) so Mastercard's issuer customers can see not just the data, but the actual sequence of steps that produced the outcome — a dimension Celonis-style tools miss because they treat network telemetry as SQL, not process. This differentiates AFPI as the collapse of the SQL+PQL two-specialist model into one Ops analyst, matching Mastercard's own AI-democratization posture. Metric: auth-approval rate improvement per issuer, false-decline rate, retention-save conversion.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Data Cloud, Financial Services Cloud

3. Merchant & Acceptance Onboarding — Network-Scale Rework Reduction

Mastercard's Commercial Acceptance Solutions team owns a very high-volume merchant/acquirer onboarding book with global compliance overlay (PCI DSS, sanctions, market-specific KYC). AFPI mines the acceptance-onboarding event log to distinguish repeatable underwriting-return loops from true one-offs, then routes the repeatable variants to Agentforce Operations. Framed against TPL, the process-mining tier surfaces the top three onboarding-drop paths within a quarter — comfortably under the cost of a single lost strategic acquirer. Metric: application-to-boarded cycle time and % applications requiring re-work.

4. Regulatory Reporting & Compliance-Center Wedge — DORA + PCI + Network Rules

Mastercard operates under DORA in the EU (ICT resilience testing, third-party register, incident classification), PCI DSS as scheme owner, plus internal network-rule enforcement across issuers and acquirers. Every one of these requires defensible "prove the process" evidence over reporting workflows. AFPI's Compliance Center gives Global Compliance and Regulatory Affairs a mined view over resilience-testing and incident-response workflows that is audit-defensible without a PQL specialist writing queries. This lands as the natural companion to the FSC Data Cloud pilot — same data, one more use case that pays back inside the quarter. Metric: reporting-cycle days, incident-classification accuracy, examiner-finding count.

5. Healthcare Payments Line-of-Business — New Data-&-Services Growth Wedge

Mastercard's Healthcare Business is a strategic growth bet within the Data & Services franchise, and healthcare-payments processes (eligibility → adjudication → patient responsibility → provider settlement) are famously variant-heavy and rework-heavy. AFPI mines the healthcare-payment lifecycle event log to expose adjudication-rework loops and hand the top variants to Agentforce Operations, giving Mastercard a repeatable "AFPI + Ethoca + Data Cloud" bundle to sell into US health systems and payers — the same collapse-two-specialists-into-one motion that makes AFPI a two-to-four-week time-to-value story. Metric: claim-to-settlement cycle time, denial-rework rate, patient-responsibility collection lift.
