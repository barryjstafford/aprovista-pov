Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. FNOL-to-Payout — Personal & Small Commercial Auto Claims

The FNOL-to-closure journey moves across digital intake, adjuster triage, coverage verification, damage estimation, vendor dispatch, subrogation, and payout — with state-DOI unfair-claims clocks running the whole time. Straight-through-processing rates hide a long tail of files cycling through reassignment and information-request loops that quietly consume most adjuster capacity. AFPI mines the actual event log across the claims platform, AI triage, and payment systems to expose the two or three loop patterns driving rework. Metric: median cycle time, LAE ratio, and DOI complaint volume.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Claims platform (Guidewire ClaimCenter or equivalent), payment / vendor dispatch system

Key Contact: Head of Claims Operations (Head of Claims Operations)

2. Small Commercial Underwriting & Quote-to-Bind

The Hartford's small commercial franchise runs on speed — the winning quote is often the first one back to the independent agent — and quote-to-bind cycle time is the single biggest lever on hit-ratio. The workflow ricochets between the agent portal, third-party data pulls (ISO, MVR, prior-carrier), the rating engine, and underwriter referrals when a rule fires. AFPI produces the true process graph and exposes which referral rules, data-vendor latencies, or agency segments structurally slow the quote. Metric: bind rate at the target SLA, and premium per underwriter FTE.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Agent portal / rating engine, third-party data-order layer

Key Contact: Head of Small Commercial Underwriting (Head of Small Commercial Underwriting)

3. Group Benefits Absence & Disability Case Management

The Hartford is a top-three US carrier in group disability, absence management, and paid family leave — a business where the "process" is a case that lives for weeks or months across intake, medical review, employer coordination, and periodic re-adjudication. State PFML mandates keep multiplying and each carries its own eligibility clock and appeal SLA. AFPI ingests the case-management event log and surfaces where cases stall against statutory clocks and which segments generate disproportionate rework. Metric: benefit-decision cycle time, appeals-overturn rate, and PFML compliance exceptions.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Case management platform, medical review system

Key Contact: Head of Group Benefits Operations (Head of Group Benefits Operations)

Top 3 Task Mining Use Cases

1. Commercial Underwriter Desktop Swivel-Chair

Commercial underwriters bounce between the agent-facing quote portal, third-party ordered data screens (ISO, MVR, loss runs), the rating engine, and referral queues to price a single risk. Task mining the underwriter desktop reveals how much time is lost to app-switching, copy/paste, and repeated lookups that Agentforce could compress into a single guided workflow. The captured user-activity log becomes the direct business case for automation. Metric: risks priced per underwriter per day and touch-time per submission.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Underwriter workbench, third-party data portals (ISO / LexisNexis)

Key Contact: Head of Commercial Underwriting Operations (Head of Commercial Underwriting Operations)

2. Claims Adjuster After-Call and Wrap-Up Time

Claims adjusters and CSRs handling FNOL, status calls, and mid-loss updates spend a material share of their day in after-call-work — updating claim notes, ordering vendor services, sending follow-up documents. Task mining captures each agent's on-screen actions to isolate the wrap steps and the tab-switching that eats handle time. This feeds a direct Agentforce for Service business case and quantifies which contact types are the best automation candidates. Metric: AHT and after-call-work minutes per contact type.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Contact-center console, claims platform

Top 3 Compliance Center Use Cases

1. SOX 404 & NAIC Model Audit Rule Attestation for Reserving

As an SEC-registered insurer, The Hartford operates under SOX Section 404 and NAIC Model Audit Rule, with reserves, DAC, and reinsurance recoverables reconstructed each year by internal audit and the external auditor — largely via interview-driven walkthroughs. Compliance Center turns the process-attestation exercise into a live artifact: the true event log across the actuarial workbench, reserving system, and financial close, with control points and segregation-of-duty breaks surfaced automatically. Metric: audit-cycle hours and SOX control-deficiency findings per reporting unit.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Actuarial workbench, reserving / GL system

Key Contact: Chief Auditor (Chief Auditor / SOX Program Lead)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
2. Multi-State DOI Unfair-Claims-Practices Controls

Every US state DOI enforces its own unfair-claims-practices act with strict statutory clocks on acknowledgement, coverage decision, and payment, plus mandatory reporting on complaints and denials. Today compliance is monitored via sample-based QA and periodic exam prep. Compliance Center codifies each state's clock as a control on the mined FNOL-to-payout event log, producing continuous evidence and auto-flagging deviations before the state does. Metric: complaint volume, exam-finding count, and time-to-evidence per DOI request.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Claims platform, complaint / regulatory case-management system

Key Contact: Chief Compliance Officer (Chief Compliance Officer)

3. Model Risk Management for AI-Driven Underwriting & Claims Triage

Predictive models drive small-commercial pricing, claims triage severity, and disability case escalation — each subject to NAIC AI/ML principles and internal model-risk management standards. Today model lifecycle governance is spreadsheet-plus-committee. Compliance Center codifies model promote / monitor / retire as a controlled workflow tied to the actual training / deployment / drift-detection event log, producing continuous evidence for internal audit and state DOI examiners. Metric: audit-ready models covered and mean-time-to-evidence.
