Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Retail Client Onboarding & KYC Refresh (Macquarie Bank / BFS). Onboarding a new retail or private-wealth client at Macquarie touches origination, identity verification, AUSTRAC AML/CTF checks, sanctions screening, and periodic KYC refresh — a process routinely audited by APRA and ASIC. AFPI mines the FSC + core banking event log to expose rework loops, aged cases waiting on document upload, and screening false-positive queues; the pain is 10-15 day median TAT vs. a fintech benchmark of <48 hours. The metric moved is straight-through-processing rate on new accounts and days-to-first-transaction. Champion: Greg Ward (Head of Banking & Financial Services Group).

2. Mortgage Origination & Broker Workflow (Macquarie Home Loans). Macquarie is the fastest-growing mortgage lender in Australia (13.1% market share, FY25) and 100% broker-distributed, which makes origination cycle time a direct competitive weapon. AFPI mines the loan-origination event log across broker submission → credit assessment → valuation → formal approval → settlement, exposing the exact hand-offs and rework causing lost deals; today Macquarie touts ~1 business day unconditional approval and needs to defend that. The metric moved is time-to-formal-approval and application withdrawal rate. Champion: Ben Perham (Head of Personal Banking).

3. Trade Lifecycle & Exceptions in Commodities & Global Markets (CGM). CGM runs a globally-distributed commodities and financial-markets trading business (energy, metals, fixed income, FX) where trade capture, confirmation, settlement, and margin management involve dozens of systems and CSDR / EMIR / Dodd-Frank / T+1 reporting obligations. AFPI mines the trade event stream to surface break patterns, aging confirmations, and manual-touch settlements before they become P&L or regulatory hits — the classic Celonis flagship use case, delivered in weeks instead of quarters. The metric moved is same-day-affirmation rate and operational risk loss events. Champion: Simon Wright (Head of CGM COO / Operations).

4. Asset Management Client Servicing & Fund Ops (MAM). Macquarie Asset Management runs A$920B AUM across public and private markets, with client onboarding, subscription/redemption processing, statement generation, and manager-of-manager oversight spread across custodians, TAs, and internal platforms. AFPI mines the client-servicing event log to expose rework in NAV escalations, late statements, and institutional client requests; the pain is retention risk on the private markets book and audit exposure. The metric moved is on-time NAV publication and institutional client NPS. Champion: Ben Way (Head of Macquarie Asset Management).

5. Complaints, Dispute Handling & Remediation (Group-wide, Compliance Center wedge). Post-Royal-Commission Australian financial-services players operate under continuous scrutiny; ASIC's RG 271 mandates strict complaints handling SLAs and Macquarie has publicly-disclosed remediation programs in-flight. AFPI + Compliance Center mines the complaint event log across intake channels, triage, root-cause, and remediation payout — producing the audit-ready process view APRA and ASIC ask for. The metric moved is % complaints resolved within RG 271 timeframe and remediation cycle time. Champion: Fiona Herbert (Chief Risk Officer, Macquarie Bank).
