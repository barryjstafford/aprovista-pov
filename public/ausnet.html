Top 3 Process Mining Use Cases

1. Bushfire-Risk Vegetation Management & Asset Inspection Cycle

Victorian ESV regulations require AusNet to inspect, cut and clear vegetation along ~50,000km of powerline corridors on rolling cycles, and delays translate directly into regulatory penalties and post-Black Saturday liability exposure. Mining the inspect → defect → work-order → contractor-dispatch → close-out flow across SAP EAM and ServiceNow surfaces rework loops and contractor bottlenecks hidden inside SLA-average reporting. AFPI overlays the GIS asset log to isolate the specific corridors where cycle time slips. Metric: % assets inspected on-cycle and mean time-to-close high-risk defects.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: SAP EAM, ServiceNow

Key Contact: Chad Hymas (Chief Operating Officer)

2. New Customer Connections & DER Onboarding

The AER benchmarks AusNet against Guaranteed Service Levels for new-connection timeliness, and rooftop-solar / battery / EV-charger applications now dwarf legacy volumes. The process spans Salesforce Industries (application intake), SAP (metering & billing), ServiceNow (field work) and GIS — classic multi-system hand-off pain. Apromore quantifies where hand-offs stall and where Agentforce can eliminate manual triage — the same wedge Origin Energy is proving today in ANZ. Metric: median days-to-connect and % applications completed without rework.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Industries, SAP

3. Outage Restoration & Storm-Response Order-to-Close

SAIDI / SAIFI reliability targets are the single largest lever in AusNet's regulated revenue determination — every extra minute of unplanned outage is monetized by the AER. The restoration process (OMS event → crew dispatch → field work → asset restoration → customer comms) crosses at least four systems and depends heavily on contractor availability. Process mining isolates storm vs. business-as-usual pathways and targets the 20% of jobs that generate 80% of outage minutes. Metric: unplanned SAIDI minutes and % of jobs restored within Guaranteed Service Level.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: GE Smallworld GIS / OMS, ServiceNow

Top 3 Compliance Center Use Cases

1. AER EDPR Evidence Chain & Regulatory Reporting

The 2026-31 Electricity Distribution Price Review (EDPR) reset requires evidence-based justification for every dollar of regulated opex/capex, and today packs are hand-assembled from SAP, ServiceNow and GIS. Compliance Center runs the mined process baseline as an AER-facing artefact and produces continuous, replayable evidence for internal audit. Catches control breaks (segregation-of-duties, safety sign-offs) before they surface in an examiner review. Metric: hours per regulatory submission and reduction in control-exception rework.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: SAP, ServiceNow

Key Contact: Mark Ellul (Chief Financial Officer)

2. ESV Bushfire Mitigation & Safety Case Conformance

Energy Safe Victoria requires AusNet to file and comply with a formal Electricity Safety Management Scheme (ESMS) — the bushfire mitigation, inspection, and defect-remediation processes must be conformant, documented, and auditable. Compliance Center encodes the safety-case control-set on top of the mined inspection process and produces always-on evidence for ESV. Removes the manual scramble every audit cycle. Metric: audit findings per control area and mean-time-to-evidence.
