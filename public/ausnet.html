Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Bushfire-Risk Vegetation Management & Asset Inspection Cycle

Victorian ESV regulations require AusNet to inspect, cut and clear vegetation along ~50,000km of powerline corridors on rolling cycles. Delays or missed inspections translate into regulatory penalties and — post-Black Saturday class action — direct liability exposure. Mining the inspect → defect → work-order → contractor-dispatch → close-out flow across SAP EAM and ServiceNow will surface rework loops and contractor bottlenecks that hide inside SLA-average reporting. Metric moved: % assets inspected on-cycle and mean time-to-close high-risk defects.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: SAP EAM, ServiceNow

Key Contact: Chad Hymas (Chief Operating Officer)

2. New Customer Connections & Distributed Energy Resource (DER) Onboarding

AER benchmarks AusNet against a Guaranteed Service Level for new-connection timeliness, and rooftop-solar / battery / EV-charger applications now dwarf legacy volumes. The process spans Salesforce (application intake), SAP (metering & billing), ServiceNow (field work) and GIS. Apromore can quantify where hand-offs stall, where re-work is triggered, and where automation with Agentforce can eliminate manual triage — the same wedge Origin Energy is proving today. Metric moved: median days-to-connect and % applications completed without rework.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Industries, SAP

Key Contact: Alistair Parker (Executive General Manager, Distribution)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
3. Outage Restoration & Storm-Response Order-to-Close

SAIDI / SAIFI reliability targets are the single largest lever in AusNet's regulated revenue determination — every extra minute of unplanned outage is directly monetized by the AER. The restoration process (OMS event → crew dispatch → field work → asset restoration → customer comms) crosses at least four systems and depends heavily on contractor availability. Process mining can isolate storm vs. business-as-usual pathways and target the 20% of jobs that generate 80% of the outage minutes. Metric moved: unplanned SAIDI minutes and % of jobs restored within Guaranteed Service Level.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: GE Smallworld GIS / OMS, ServiceNow

Key Contact: Justin Harding (Chief Information & Digital Officer)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
4. Field Service Dispatch & Contractor Work Management

AusNet runs a hybrid workforce — internal crews plus a heavy contractor spend (Downer, Zinfra, Ventia) — and the pending "AusNet - Strategic Field Mobility" $600K opportunity is explicitly aimed at this pain. Mining the dispatch-to-invoice loop across SAP, ServiceNow and Salesforce Field Service will expose double-handling, incorrect crew skilling, and invoice-reconciliation rework. This directly de-risks the Field Mobility deal and gives the Agentforce Contact Centre opp a quantified baseline. Metric moved: right-first-time %, average productive hours per crew day.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Salesforce Field Service, SAP

Key Contact: Chad Hymas (Chief Operating Officer)

5. Regulatory Reporting & EDPR Compliance Evidence Chain

The 2026-31 Electricity Distribution Price Review (EDPR) reset lands mid-cycle for AusNet, and the AER requires evidence-based justification for every dollar of regulated opex/capex. Today, evidence packs are hand-assembled from SAP, ServiceNow and GIS — the classic Compliance-Center wedge. Apromore can generate a mined, replayable process baseline that becomes the AER-facing artefact, and the same conformance model catches control breaks (segregation-of-duties, safety sign-offs) before an internal audit does. Metric moved: hours per regulatory submission and reduction in control-exception rework.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: SAP, ServiceNow
