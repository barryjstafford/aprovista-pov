Top 5 Process Intelligence Use Cases to be Mined

1. Rare-Disease Patient Access & Onboarding (Chiesi Global Rare Diseases / ex-Amryt)

Since acquiring Amryt Pharma in 2023, Chiesi Global Rare Diseases runs patient-support programmes for ultra-rare conditions (Myalept, Filsuvez, Mycapssa) where time-to-therapy directly changes outcomes and every case touches enrollment, benefits investigation, prior authorisation, specialty-pharmacy dispense, adherence and adverse-event reporting. AFPI mines the enrollment-to-first-dose event log across Health Cloud, specialty-pharmacy interfaces and PSP call-centre queues to expose which prior-auth denial patterns, benefits-verification loops or hub handoffs are stretching cycle time. Feeding that map into the already-scoped "Chiesi Rare Disease Agentforce" pilot lets an agent triage the exact steps proven to slip. Metric moved: median time-to-first-dose, prior-auth turnaround, and PSP abandonment rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Health Cloud (Amryt org), Service Cloud Call Center

Key Contact: Giacomo Chiesi (Head of Chiesi Global Rare Diseases)

2. Regulatory Submission & Response Cycle Time (R&D)

The open "Chiesi - Agent R&D Regulatory" opp signals an active push to compress regulatory submission and health-authority response handling — a process where every EMA/FDA question, CTA amendment, labelling update and IDMP data change cascades through Regulatory Affairs, Medical Writing, PV and Legal. Today those handoffs live in email, Veeva Vault and shared drives with variants no one has ever mapped. AFPI mines the submission-and-response event log to reveal which question types, sites or product families cause the most rework, so Agentforce can pre-triage them. Metric moved: mean submission cycle time, response-turnaround SLA compliance, and rework loops per dossier.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Veeva Vault RIM (TBD), Salesforce R&D Regulatory workflow

3. Tibco→NTT Integration Cutover & Post-Cutover Process Stability

The open "MS - Chiesi - CTOh Tibco repl with NTT" opp is replacing Tibco with an NTT-delivered integration platform — the kind of programme that either lands with hidden fallout (silently-dropped events, mis-mapped variants, duplicate cases) or with a defensible baseline the business trusts. AFPI mines the "as-is" event flow across Tibco endpoints today and re-mines the "to-be" flow on NTT after cutover, giving the CIO office an objective before/after conformance and throughput view rather than a red/green dashboard. Metric moved: message-drop rate, end-to-end order/patient/complaint latency, and post-cutover incident volume. [Slack] Confirmed active in Org62 as Stage 02 - Scoping.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Tibco (in flight), NTT integration platform

Key Contact: Luigi Ballerini (Group CIO, Chiesi)

4. Complaint Handling & CAPA (Pharmacovigilance + Quality)

Chiesi's respiratory portfolio (Foster, Trimbow) and rare-disease portfolio generate a steady stream of product complaints and adverse-event reports that must be logged, investigated, categorised and — where warranted — feed CAPA within GxP-defined windows. Missed windows drive Form 483 exposure and warning-letter risk. AFPI mines the complaint-to-closure and CAPA-to-effectiveness-check event log to expose where investigator hand-offs, source-verification loops or effectiveness-check reopens compound cycle time, and to prove which complaint categories deserve which target investigation depth. Metric moved: complaint-cycle SLA compliance, CAPA closure-on-time rate, and repeat-defect rate.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Veeva Vault QMS/Safety (TBD), Service Cloud Call Center

Key Contact: Alessandro Chiesi (Head of Europe Region, Chiesi Farmaceutici)

Deep Dive (coming soon)
5. HCP Engagement, MSL Response & Medical Information Handling

Across the 30+ Chiesi country affiliates, HCP outreach lives in Sales Cloud/Marketing Cloud, medical-information requests hit Service Cloud Call Center, and MSL follow-ups often re-enter through separate channels — so a single HCP conversation is fragmented across systems. AFPI mines the HCP-touch event log to reveal which country affiliates convert an inquiry into a productive MSL/AE follow-up fastest, where compliant-off-label response variants stall, and where duplicate outreach wastes rep time. Feeding that pattern into Agentforce lets the medical-info agent pre-route the low-risk questions and route the high-value HCPs to the right human. Metric moved: medical-info response SLA, MSL follow-up conversion, and rep-touch efficiency by country.

Key System(s) to be Process Mined: Sales Cloud, Service Cloud Call Center
