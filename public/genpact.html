Why Not Now: Genpact is a T3 Nurture, not a T1/T2 pursuit. As a global BPM/professional-services firm, Genpact is deeply embedded in the process-mining ecosystem — they are one of the largest Celonis global implementation partners and have built a substantial services practice around it. Selling AFPI to Genpact for their own internal use would compete directly with an active partner relationship they monetize with clients. In addition, Genpact's own Cora AI/automation platform overlaps conceptually with parts of the AFPI value proposition. Combined with the fact that they run client operations rather than transformative internal process change, this reduces urgency and creates a material competitive-posture red flag.

What Would Need to Change to Move to T2

  • Cora + Agentforce co-innovation: A partnership conversation positioning AFPI as an embedded intelligence layer Genpact resells inside Cora to their end-customers — not a rip-and-replace of Celonis internally. This flips the motion from competitive to complementary and unlocks a much larger TCV.
  • Salesforce Agentforce operational deployment: If Genpact expands the current Agentic Marketing Platform opp into a broader Agentforce Operations rollout (contact center, F&A shared services, procurement), AFPI becomes the natural process-discovery layer that finds where agents should act. This is the most credible near-term wedge.
  • Client-facing offering: A signed OEM/embed motion where AFPI ships inside Genpact's Digital Operations service catalog would move this to T1 quickly — but that is a corporate-development conversation, not an AE-led one.

Signals Worth Monitoring

  • Agentforce expansion in the current $135k opp — watch for it to broaden beyond Marketing Cloud into Service or Sales Cloud agentic use cases; that is the trigger to layer AFPI in.
  • Genpact quarterly earnings commentary on AI-led revenue mix — when BK Kalra (CEO) or Mike Weiner (CFO) begin narrating a shift from headcount-based BPM to outcome-based agentic services, the technology-partner conversation reopens.
  • Any RFP for internal Order-to-Cash or Procure-to-Pay transformation issued by Genpact's own corporate function (not a client engagement) — extremely rare, but if it lands, it is a valid AFPI wedge on business-analyst simplicity vs. their current tooling.
  • Slack #apromore-gtm mentions — currently zero. Track weekly for any partner or opportunity chatter tied to Genpact.

Quarterly touchpoint only. Do not push a standalone AFPI proposal. Coordinate with the Salesforce Agentforce AE currently working the $135k Marketing opp — if that opp progresses to closed-won and expands, that is the correct moment to introduce AFPI as an operational-discovery layer for the next phase.
