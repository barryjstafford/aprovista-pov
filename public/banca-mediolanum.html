1. Family Banker Client-Onboarding & KYC (Retail Wealth)

Banca Mediolanum's differentiator is its ~6,000 Family Bankers advising retail wealth clients across Italy, Germany, Spain, and Ireland — but that model turns onboarding into a many-hop process across advisor app, KYC engine, custody, and MDM. AFPI mines the client-to-first-investment journey to expose where documents get re-requested, where AML/PEP checks bounce back, and where advisors abandon in favor of manual work-arounds. With Informatica MDM already governing the golden customer record, the event log is unusually clean and ready to mine. Metric moved: onboarding cycle time, advisor drop-off rate, first-investment time-to-fund.

As an ECB Significant Institution operating retail deposits, insurance, and asset management across four EU jurisdictions, Mediolanum files a large volume of Segnalazioni Antiriciclaggio and is directly in DORA's operational-resilience scope. AFPI mines the transaction-monitoring alert-to-disposition-to-SAR-filing process to quantify investigator variance, escalation rework, and SLA breaches; Compliance Center then produces the evidenced control trail that Banca d'Italia inspectors and DORA regulators expect on demand. 3. Cross-Border Operating-Model Harmonization (IT / DE / ES / IE)

Banca Mediolanum operates the same "Family Banker" model across four legal entities — Banca Mediolanum (IT), Bankhaus August Lenz (DE), Banco Mediolanum (Spain), and the Irish life/asset entity — but each stack has grown its own onboarding, complaint, and product-transfer variations. AFPI mines the same golden process across all four subsidiaries to quantify variance, expose which country's flow is best-in-class, and generate a data-backed harmonization roadmap. This directly supports the cost-to-serve and CET1 story to the ECB. Metric moved: process-variance rate across subsidiaries, cost-per-transaction gap, harmonization-milestone slippage.

4. Post-Mediobanca-Divestment Treasury & Reporting Reshape

In June 2025 Banca Mediolanum divested its full 3.5% stake in Mediobanca via accelerated bookbuild, freeing roughly €548M in capital and forcing a reshape of treasury reporting, investment governance, and ECB CET1 disclosures.
